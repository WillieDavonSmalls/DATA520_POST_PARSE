  <div class="vtbegenerated">
   <p>As investors look for alternative data sources to provide them with the "edge", one area that is receiving a lot of attention is using NLP to perform sentiment analysis of financial report filings by publicly traded companies with the SEC.</p> 
   <p>I found this notebook by Roshan Adusumilli&nbsp;(Github&nbsp;link here:&nbsp; <a href="https://github.com/roshan-adusumilli/nlp_10-ks/blob/master/NLP_on_Financial_Statements.ipynb">https://github.com/roshan-adusumilli/nlp_10-ks/blob/master/NLP_on_Financial_Statements.ipynb</a>) quite interesting because of a few different reasons:</p> 
   <p>1) He is a 16 year old.</p> 
   <p>2) He explains the different steps in NLP quite well.</p> 
   <p>3) His notebook&nbsp;also shows how to download 10-K filings from the SEC website, so it's not just about NLP.</p> 
   <p>The corresponding article that walks through his process is here (<a href="https://towardsdatascience.com/nlp-in-the-stock-market-8760d062eb92">https://towardsdatascience.com/nlp-in-the-stock-market-8760d062eb92</a>).</p> 
   <p>In terms of improvements, I cannot think of anything significant. Perhaps he could have analyzed more thoroughly, whether the sentiment analysis could be used to predict the sock price return on the date of filing.</p>
