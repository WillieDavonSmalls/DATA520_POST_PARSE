  <div class="vtbegenerated">
   <p style="margin: 0.0in;font-family: Calibri;font-size: 11.0pt;">I found 2 articles (one siting the other), which describe controversies around Uber's restrictions of its data.&nbsp; There may not be as much limitation on using Uber's data for some downstream analysis, as oppose to the limitations as to how you can use this data and the type of products you are allowed to develop based on this data.&nbsp; In the article, Uber cut off access to its API for a Boston based startup which was looking to provide price comparisons between major ride-hailing services.&nbsp; There's a possibility that this move by Uber could violate antitrust law.&nbsp; Ironically, Uber itself relies on 3rd party API for maps, payments and messaging.</p> 
   <p style="margin: 0.0in;font-family: Calibri;font-size: 11.0pt;">This article essentially demonstrates that while a 3rd party, private data provider, like Uber, may increase or promote a wider use of its data, it may also decrease or impose on the scope of what can be built on top of its data.</p>
