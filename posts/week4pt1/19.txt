  <div class="vtbegenerated">
   <div>
     A friend of mine is an author and regularly connects with four other authors via social media and occasional lunches.&nbsp; Last week she shared that two other members of the group were both contacted by a publisher and asked if they'd like to write a book for them.&nbsp; I have no idea how the publisher goes about identifying topics, or authors they'd like to have write for them, but here's what it might look like if they chose to do so via network data analysis and text processing...&nbsp; 
   </div> 
   <div></div> 
   <div> 
    <b>Business Goal:</b> Equip book publishers with a tool/model that enables them to identify topics of high interest to the American people and authors to reach out to write the books. 
   </div> 
   <div></div> 
   <div> 
    <b>Approach:</b> 
   </div> 
   <div>
     1. Identify topics that are of... 
   </div> 
   <blockquote style="padding: 0.0px;margin-top: 0.0px;margin-right: 0.0px;margin-bottom: 0.0px;" dir="ltr"> 
    <div>
      highest interest (threshold to-be-determined) across America 
    </div> 
    <div>
      identify if a lot or a little (threshold to-be determined) has been written already about the topic 
    </div> 
    <div></div> 
   </blockquote> 
   <div>
     2. Identify authors that... 
   </div> 
   <blockquote style="padding: 0.0px;margin-top: 0.0px;margin-right: 0.0px;margin-bottom: 0.0px;" dir="ltr"> 
    <div>
      have expertise (criteria to-be-determined) in the topics identified 
    </div> 
   </blockquote> 
   <blockquote style="padding: 0.0px;margin-top: 0.0px;margin-right: 0.0px;margin-bottom: 0.0px;" dir="ltr"> 
    <div>
      have an expansive (threshold to-be-determined) social network 
    </div> 
   </blockquote> 
   <div></div> 
   <div>
     3. Marry steps 1 and 2 together to identify books to write and authors to write them 
   </div> 
   <div></div> 
   <div> 
    <b>Potential sources of data:</b> 
   </div> 
   <div> 
    <i>Topics:</i> 
   </div> 
   <div>
     1. Social Media (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, etc...) posts 
   </div> 
   <div>
     2. Main-stream media headlines and articles 
   </div> 
   <div></div> 
   <div> 
    <i>Authors:</i> 
   </div> 
   <div>
     3. New York Times and other best seller lists 
   </div> 
   <div>
     4. Amazon ranks books online across categories, so successful authors could be identified 
   </div> 
   <div>
     5. Facebook followers to assess social network 
   </div> 
   <br>
  </div>
 
</div>
