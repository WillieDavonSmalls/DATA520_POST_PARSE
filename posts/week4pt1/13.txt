  <div class="vtbegenerated">
   <p>In this day of sensationalized news it is often very interesting to trace the life of a story. For the more reputable sources it usually starts with a significant amount of leg work,&nbsp;primary research and in-depth interviews. Publications with integrity will wait until they have at least 3 independent sources corroborating a story before publishing.&nbsp;</p> 
   <p>Newer media, however, does not hold to these lofty standards. A story you see on some news channels will at times be the result of a "reporter" seeing a tweet or a blog&nbsp;post and running with the story. Little or no fact-checking is done since the point of the story is internet&nbsp;traffic, not educating the consumer. With social media such stories can get caught up in a retweet&nbsp;or repost craze that spreads the story far and wide with many of the readers not even questioning the validity of the information.&nbsp;</p> 
   <p>I can envision a system that scrapes news articles for quotes, headlines, punchlines (basically key predictors) and developing networks of connected stories to identify trends in the way sources/information are reused. These networks could then be analyzed to identify echo chambers&nbsp; as well as islands of diversified information. The business goal of such an endeavor would be to provide an objective evaluation of the quality of sourcing used by a news organization. Each network would be scored based on the connections it maintains and the sources it uses.&nbsp;</p>
